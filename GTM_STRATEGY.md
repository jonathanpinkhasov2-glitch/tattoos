# TattooOS — Go-to-Market Strategy

## What Is TattooOS?

TattooOS is practice management software built specifically for tattoo artists and studios. Think of it as the tool every tattooist wishes existed: one place to handle bookings, collect deposits, get consent forms signed, manage clients, and track revenue — without juggling Instagram DMs, Google Forms, Venmo, and Calendly at the same time.

**Live product:** https://tattooos-sand.vercel.app

---

## The Problem

The average tattoo artist wastes 2–4 hours every week on admin:

- Chasing clients through Instagram DMs for booking details
- Manually invoicing deposits via Venmo/CashApp and hoping clients pay
- Printing paper consent forms or using generic Google Forms
- Losing track of client history, reference images, and notes
- No-shows because there's no reminder system

Most artists use a chaotic stack: Instagram for inquiries, Calendly for scheduling, Venmo for deposits, Google Forms for consent, and Notes/spreadsheets for client tracking. None of it talks to each other. TattooOS replaces all of it.

---

## Who It's For

**Primary target:** Independent tattoo artists earning $3K–$15K/month who do 10–40 sessions/month. These artists have enough volume to feel the pain but aren't large enough to have office staff.

**Secondary target:** Small studios (2–6 artists) that want shared scheduling and studio-level analytics.

**Not the target (yet):** Walk-in shops, large franchise studios, or artists doing fewer than 4 sessions/month.

---

## Why Artists Should Use TattooOS

### The pitch in one sentence:
> "Stop managing your business through DMs. TattooOS handles bookings, deposits, and consent forms so you can focus on the work."

### Specific value props:

**1. Booking link you can actually share**
One link. Client fills out tattoo details, picks a date, submits. You get notified. No back-and-forth.

**2. Deposits collected automatically**
Set a flat amount or percentage. Clients pay via Stripe when you confirm the booking. Money goes directly to your bank. No chasing.

**3. Digital consent forms**
Send a link, client signs on their phone. Stored forever, searchable by name. No more clipboards, no lost paper.

**4. Client history in one place**
Every client's bookings, reference photos, notes, allergies, and spending history — all in one profile. When they rebook, you know exactly what you worked on.

**5. Automatic reminders**
24h and 2h SMS/email reminders reduce no-shows without you lifting a finger.

**6. Revenue dashboard**
See exactly what you've earned, what deposits are outstanding, and what's coming up — without doing math in your head.

## Go-to-Market Plan

### Phase 1: First 10 Paying Customers (Weeks 1–4)

The goal is proof of concept. Get 10 artists paying $39/month = $390 MRR.

**Step 1: Find artists who already feel the pain**

Go to Instagram and search:
- `#tattooartist` + city (start with: NYC, LA, Miami, Chicago, Austin, Atlanta)
- Look for artists with 2K–50K followers (big enough to be busy, small enough to care about DMs)
- Find artists who post things like "DM to book" or "booking open" — these are the ones using manual processes

**Step 2: Send 50 cold DMs per day**

The DM that works:
> "Hey [name] — I built a booking tool specifically for tattoo artists. It handles the whole flow: client books online, pays deposit automatically, signs consent form digitally. Free for 2 weeks, takes 5 min to set up. Want to try it? tattooos-sand.vercel.app"

Keep it short. Don't pitch features. Offer the trial. Response rate target: 5–10%.

**Step 3: Hop on a 15-min call with anyone who responds**

You're not selling — you're onboarding. Ask them to share screen. Walk them through setting up their booking page. This call = they become a customer.

**Step 4: Get a testimonial from each early user**

After week 1: "What's one thing TattooOS saved you time on?" Screenshot the answer. Use it on the landing page.

---

### Phase 2: Scale to 100 Customers (Months 2–3)

**Channel 1: Instagram content**

Post from a dedicated TattooOS account:
- Before/after: "Managing bookings before TattooOS vs after" (screenshot comparison)
- Short Reels showing the booking form being filled out
- Artist testimonials / screen recordings

Target: 1 post/day, 3 Reels/week. Use hashtags: `#tattooartist #tattoolife #tattoobusiness #tattoobooking`

**Channel 2: TikTok**

Tattoo content performs exceptionally well on TikTok. Post:
- "I built software for tattoo artists and this is how it works" (founder story)
- Screen recordings of the product with voiceover
- "POV: your booking page looks like this" demos

**Channel 3: Reddit**

Post in:
- r/tattooartists (5K+ members)
- r/TattooApprentice
- r/startups (founder journey posts)

Don't spam. Post genuinely useful content ("How I reduced no-shows by 80%"), mention TattooOS naturally.

**Channel 4: Direct outreach to tattoo supply stores**

Email or call 10 tattoo supply shops/distributors (Kingpin, Painful Pleasures, Electrum, etc.). Ask to be mentioned in their artist newsletters or partner discounts. These shops have direct relationships with thousands of artists.

**Channel 5: Tattoo conventions**

Research the next major convention in a driveable city (Hell City, Baltimore Tattoo, Empire State, etc.). Set up a booth or simply attend and hand out business cards with the free trial URL. Artists are in the same place. This is dense pipeline.

---

### Phase 3: $10K MRR (Month 4–6)

At $39/seat average, $10K MRR = ~256 paying artists.

**Unlock referral program**
Every paying artist gets a referral link. If they bring in another artist who pays for 2 months, they get one month free. Tattoo artists trust other tattoo artists. This is the highest-conversion channel once you have an initial base.

**YouTube / long-form content**
"The complete guide to running a tattoo business" — target keyword-rich search terms that artists look up when frustrated with their current setup.

**Studio plans**
Start targeting studio owners. One studio = 4 artists paying $79 total vs. $156 for 4 solo plans. Better unit economics for them, higher ACV for you. Email/cold outreach to studio Instagram accounts.

**Press**
Pitch to:
- Tattoo-specific media: Inked Magazine, Tattoo Life
- Small business media: The Hustle, Morning Brew, Starter Story (founder story angle)
- Local press: "Local developer builds app for tattoo artists in [city]"

## The Exit Path

At $300K–$500K ARR (target acquisition range):

**Likely acquirers:**
- **Square / Block** — already in salon/beauty vertical, tattoo is adjacent
- **Vagaro** — would pay to acquire tattoo-specific market share rather than build
- **Mindbody** — moving into more verticals
- **Private equity rollups** — beauty/wellness SaaS rollups like Zenoti

**Valuation:** SaaS businesses at this stage typically exit at 4–6x ARR. At $400K ARR you're looking at $1.6M–$2.4M.

**What makes this attractive to acquirers:**
1. Vertical specificity — hard to replicate the trust and product fit
2. Sticky revenue — once an artist's client list and history is in TattooOS, they don't leave
3. Network effects — the referral program creates clusters of artists at the same studios
